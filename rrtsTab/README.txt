This is the implementation of the RRT algorithms provided in Dart. The demonstration of the 
classical algorithms are done by Ana Huaman and Tobias Kunz. The taks constrained examples
are by Can Erdogan.

2013-01-30

List of algorithms:

Classics:

1- Baseline RRT ~
   LaValle, Steven M. "Rapidly-Exploring Random Trees A New Tool for Path Planning." (1998).

2- Goal-biased, bidirectional RRT ~
   LaValle, Steven M., and James J. Kuffner Jr. "Rapidly-exploring random trees: Progress and 
   prospects." (2000).

3- RRT Connect ~
   Kuffner Jr, James J., and Steven M. LaValle. "RRT-connect: An efficient approach to single-query 
   path planning." Robotics and Automation, 2000. Proceedings. ICRA'00. IEEE International 
   Conference on. Vol. 2. IEEE, 2000.

4- RRT Path shortening - Corner-cut method ~
   Chen, Pang C., and Yong K. Hwang. "SANDROS: a dynamic graph search algorithm for motion 
   planning." Robotics and Automation, IEEE Transactions on 14.3 (1998): 390-403.
   
Task Costrained Motion Planning:

5- Randomized Gradient Descent ~
   Lavalle, Steven M., Jeffery H. Yakey, and Lydia E. Kavraki. "A probabilistic roadmap approach for 
   systems with closed kinematic chains." Robotics and Automation, 1999. Proceedings. 1999 
   IEEE International Conference on. Vol. 3. IEEE, 1999.

6- Tangent Space Sampling ~
   Stilman, Mike. "Task constrained motion planning in robot joint space." Intelligent Robots and 
   Systems, 2007. IROS 2007. IEEE/RSJ International Conference on. IEEE, 2007.

7- First-Order Retraction ~
   Stilman, Mike. "Task constrained motion planning in robot joint space." Intelligent Robots and 
   Systems, 2007. IROS 2007. IEEE/RSJ International Conference on. IEEE, 2007.

8- Soft Task Constraints ~
   Kunz, Tobias, and Mike Stilman. "Manipulation planning with soft task constraints." Intelligent 
   Robots and Systems (IROS), 2012 IEEE/RSJ International Conference on. IEEE, 2012.
